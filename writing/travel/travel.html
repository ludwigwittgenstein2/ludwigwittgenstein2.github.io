  • 16 Jan 2017 Tamil Nadu


    Most parts of Tamil Nadu looks the same in terms of Architecture, except for the weather.
    Some great facts about Tamil culture is that, I found Sangam period as a foundation of Tamil culture, literature. They had worthy moral precepts, stories. However, it is not popular in the West -- I think, they are worth studying and spreading wisdom and knowledge.



    Kodaikanal, Tamil Nadu, India.



    Kodaikanal is known as princess of hill station. A Hill station is where we have a village near the top of a hill. The Weather is around 70 degrees to 60 degrees. It formed as a retreat place for Missionaries, British elites in Tamil Nadu. People in Tamil Nadu soon started to see it as a wonderful place, and started relocating to Kodaikanal. I have been to Kodaikanal numerous times, And -- it is one of my favorite places on the planet. If you plan to visit Tamil Nadu, I recommend staying in Kodaikanal for a week. You will enjoy the perfect weather, scenery and forest.



    Tirunelveli, Tamil Nadu, India.




    Tirunelveli has semi-arid weather.
    Semi-arid, mostly hot with 10-20 days of rainfall a year

    It's a small town with about 500,000 people living in the district.
    There's a popular destination called, Coutrallam.
    It has few waterfalls, where people often go for a small retreat.
    I've been to coutrallam many times, And I fall in love with majestic water-fall.

    Tirunelveli has excellent middle and high schools. They call it the Oxford of South India.
    Most of the credit goes to early Christian missionaries who founded these schools.
    Other than that, there's not a complete history of Tirunelveli.
    If you find something let me know.

    Coimbatore

    Coimbatore has 70 degrees weather, and rains often.
    However, the city has numerous migrants from other parts of India.
    Coimbatore also has excellent schools, and institutions.
    Close to Coimbatore, there's Ooty, which is similar to Kodaikanal.
    I've visited it three times. The Railway station is peculiar, and has an underground walk-way.


    Chennai Madras

    Chennai is probably bigger than NYC, and is capital of Tamil Nadu. It's hot, humid and has a lot of traffic.
    I would say NYC's traffic is nothing compared to Chennai.
    During my time in Chennai, I saw how fast Chennai was growing economically.
    It's interesting to visit a crowded city if you are young, And Chennai has lot of things to do. If you are into History, I'd recommend checking out all the English East India company's history in Chennai.
    A lot of people in Chennai are proud of it. It was formerly called as Madras.
    The first time, I visited Chennai was in 2008 during vacation
    After that, I moved to Chennai for four years.

    Madurai

    Madurai is 150 kms from Tirunelveli. The City of Madurai's icon is Meenakshi Amman Temple, if you have not seen it. Find out the details of architecture, it's unique in History. I'd say Dravidian Architecture is unique and has stone-cut temples. Other than that, there's other temples, people.

    Trivanduram, Kerala


    Trivanduram, I visited it from my early schooling days. It's similar to other Tamil Nadu towns, cities. The Weather is not too hot as Tirunelveli. It's around 80 degrees. People speak Malayalam in Kerala. If you have not noticed, people in India speak different language, depending on the state.
    Kottayam, Kerala

    Kottayam was greener and I enjoyed my time visiting on a train.

    New Delhi

    I was so much excited to be in New Delhi.

    And as soon as I got from a train, I could see people from all over India were there.
    Remember, India is diverse as Europe.

    And that makes India special. India was doomed to fail after the British had left,
    however -- India is still going on as a Nation. Anyway, I noticed
    clean/good areas of New Delhi were near consulates,
    and I must admit they had poorer parts too in New Delhi.
    A lot of the signs in New Delhi were in Urdu (remember), this was surprising.
    I think, it goes back to the Mughal Empire.
    I visited some of the bazaars in New Delhi, they were crowded with tons of people.
    Infact, I was traveling alone so I couldn't get much feedback from locals.
    I wish -- I had stayed there for a while to learn about the place.


    Gurgaon, Haryana.
    From New Delhi, there's trains to Gurgaon. It is similar to New Delhi, but new thing I noticed was that Northern India has rickshaws, and they called their three-wheeled transport vehicle as fat fatia. Probably in Southern India, I have not seen them, and people in Tamil Nadu called them as Autos.



    Tajmahal, Agra.



    What can I say about the Taj Mahal? Majestic architecture, grandeur, opulent, symbol of Mughal Empire.Taj Mahal is a must-visit in India
    Infact, if you love history or architecture -- Then you'll fall in love with it.

    Middle East





    In fact, this place was fascinatingly different. The first difference is clothing style, next is weather. I couldn't research much on the history of it because I couldn't talk to the locals. Although, I must warn people who visit Middle East for the hot weather. It gets as hot as 122 degrees. I loved the Desert, it looks different than Europe or North America.

    Fiji



    Fiji is a great place to visit, you would love the weather in summer.
    People are friendly. Fun fact, there are Indians in Fiji, who have assimilated to Fijian culture.
    Fijians would belong to Islander culture.



    Thailand
    Bangkok Pattaya
    Thailand has friendly people. A lot of travel and tourism in Thailand.
    The first you might notice in Bangkok is that it's a huge city similar to NYC. Bangkok has dozens of Buddhist temples. I don't think Thailand has winters, they have a lot of things to do in Thailand.

If you like small places, with less people, you might enjoy it unless, you can't stand rural places.
